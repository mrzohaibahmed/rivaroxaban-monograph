medical_information Identifiers

Brand Names: Xarelto, Rivarox, Rixaban

Chemical Name: 5-Chloro-N-[(5S)-2-oxo-3-[4-(3-oxo-4-morpholinyl)phenyl]-5-oxazolidinyl]methyl]-2-thiophenecarboxamide

Molecular Formula: C19H18ClN3O5S

Class: Direct Factor Xa Inhibitor

info Introduction

Rivaroxaban is an oral anticoagulant; a direct, activated factor X (Xa) inhibitor. Patented in 2007 and approved in the United States in 2011, it is available as a generic medication and is listed on the World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines. In 2022, it ranked as the 90th most commonly prescribed medication in the U.S. with over 7 million prescriptions.

science Mechanism of Action

Rivaroxaban selectively inhibits Factor Xa, an essential enzyme in the coagulation cascade responsible for converting prothrombin to thrombin, thereby preventing fibrin clot formation.

assignment_turned_in Indications and Dose
Prophylaxis of venous thromboembolism following knee replacement surgery:

Adult: 10 mg once daily for 2 weeks, started 6–10 hours post-surgery.

Prophylaxis of venous thromboembolism following hip replacement surgery:

Adult: 10 mg once daily for 5 weeks, started 6–10 hours post-surgery.

Treatment of deep-vein thrombosis; pulmonary embolism:

Adult: 15 mg twice daily for 21 days with food, then maintenance 20 mg once daily with food.

Consider long-term treatment for high risk or previous recurrence.
Prophylaxis of recurrent deep-vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism:

Adult: 20 mg once daily with food.

Prophylaxis of stroke and systemic embolism in non-valvular atrial fibrillation patients:

Adult: 20 mg once daily with food. Reduce to 15 mg if moderate renal impairment.

Prophylaxis of atherothrombotic events (with aspirin):

2.5 mg twice daily, usually for 12 months.

Off-Label Uses
  • Treatment of acute heparin-induced thrombocytopenia in stable patients.
  • Post-percutaneous coronary intervention with stent placement in nonvalvular atrial fibrillation.
  • Acute, symptomatic superficial vein thrombosis.
  • Cerebral venous sinus thrombosis (CVST).
  • Cancer-associated thrombosis (CAT).
  • Extended thromboprophylaxis in high-risk orthopedic cases.
  • Patients with left ventricular thrombus (limited data).
medication Pharmacokinetics
  • Time to peak plasma concentration: 2–4 hours
  • Protein binding: 92–95%
  • Half-life: 5–9 hours (young), 11–13 hours (elderly)
warning Important Safety Information
Risk in Antiphospholipid Syndrome (APS): Not recommended for APS patients with history of blood clots or triple positive markers; consider vitamin K antagonists instead.
Bleeding Risk & Food Requirements: Doses of 15 mg or 20 mg must be taken with food for proper absorption.
Patient Counseling: Monitor for serious bleeding signs (black stools, blood in urine, severe headaches, dizziness). Educate on bleeding risk and taking medication with food.
report Black Box Warning
  • Epidural or spinal hematomas may occur, possibly causing permanent paralysis. Risk increases with epidural catheters, concomitant anticoagulants, NSAIDs, or spinal surgery/trauma.
  • Early stopping increases thrombotic risk; consult medical guidance.
pregnant_woman Pregnancy Category: C
Use only if benefits justify fetal risks. Crosses placenta, increasing bleeding risk. Women of reproductive age should discuss plans, especially with abnormal bleeding or needing long-term anticoagulation.
block Contraindications
  • Active bleeding-antiphospholipid syndrome
  • Severe renal impairment (CrCl <15 mL/min)
  • Hepatic disease with coagulopathy
  • Hypersensitivity to rivaroxaban
  • Recent brain, spinal, ophthalmic, or hip fracture surgery

inventory_2 Storage

Tablets: Store at room temperature, 25ºC (77ºF); excursions permitted to 15-30ºC (59-86ºF).
Crushed tablets in water or applesauce: Store at room temperature, 25ºC (77ºF) for up to 4 hours.

medication Antidote

Andexanet alfa (FDA-approved) or Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (PCC) may be administered for life-threatening bleeding.
